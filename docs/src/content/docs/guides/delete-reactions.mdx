---
title: Delete Reactions
description: Remove your reactions alongside message cleanup.
---

Reaction cleanup is enabled by default in `dmd clean`.

Use [`--keep-reactions`](../../reference/cli-options/#keep-reactions) only when you want to opt out and keep your own reactions.

## What it does

When the tool encounters a message in scope, it can remove normal reactions and Super Reactions that belong to you if the message itself is not being deleted.

## Important behavior

- If a message will be deleted, its reactions are not treated as separate work items.
- Deleting your message also deletes normal and Super Reactions that other users attached to it. Dry-run reports that foreign-reaction impact separately.
- If a message is kept and reaction cleanup is active, your reactions on that message can still be removed.
- Discord reports normal ownership as `me` and Super Reaction ownership as `me_burst`; DMD plans the two variants independently, including when both use the same emoji.
- Use `--keep-reactions` if you want kept messages to keep your reactions as well.
- Reaction removal is always about your own reactions, not other users' reactions.
- Reaction removal is skipped in archived threads because Discord restricts mutations there.

## Example

```bash
dmd clean --include-ids <id> --dry-run
```

In dry-run mode, the summary shows:

- your separately planned reaction deletions and preserved reactions
- foreign normal and Super Reactions affected by deleting your parent messages

Discord's documented Reaction object supplies integer `count`, `count_details.normal`, and `count_details.burst` fields plus boolean `me` and `me_burst` ownership flags. DMD verifies that the total equals the normal and Super Reaction breakdown, subtracts the authenticated user's instances, and reports the remaining exact count at scan time. A missing, malformed, or inconsistent required field produces `unknown`, not an estimate.

## When to use it

Default cleanup already covers both:

- your message history
- your reaction history

If you want to understand how the timing and summaries are presented, see [Log Output](../../reference/log-output/).
